# CKA Exam Overview

The Certified Kubernetes Administrator (CKA) exam is a professional certification that evaluates your knowledge and skills in managing Kubernetes clusters. This guide aims to provide a comprehensive overview of the exam structure, key topics, and effective study strategies.

## Exam Structure

- **Format**: The CKA exam is online, proctored, and consists of multiple performance-based tasks.
- **Duration**: You will have 2 hours to complete the exam.
- **Passing Score**: The minimum passing score is 66%.

## Key Topics Covered

1. **Cluster Architecture, Installation & Configuration**
   - Understanding Kubernetes architecture
   - Installing and configuring a Kubernetes cluster

2. **Workloads & Scheduling**
   - Managing Pods, Deployments, and ReplicaSets
   - Scheduling and administration of workloads

3. **Services & Networking**
   - Configuring Services and Ingress
   - Networking concepts in Kubernetes

4. **Storage**
   - Persistent Volumes and Persistent Volume Claims
   - Storage classes and dynamic provisioning

5. **Troubleshooting**
   - Diagnosing and resolving issues in a Kubernetes cluster

6. **RBAC (Role-Based Access Control)**
   - Understanding roles, role bindings, and permissions
   - Implementing RBAC for secure access control

## Study Tips

- **Hands-On Practice**: Set up a local Kubernetes cluster using tools like Minikube or Kind to practice commands and configurations.
- **Documentation**: Familiarize yourself with the official Kubernetes documentation, as it is a valuable resource during the exam.
- **Practice Exams**: Take practice exams to simulate the test environment and improve your time management skills.
- **Join Study Groups**: Collaborate with peers or participate in online forums to discuss topics and share resources.

By following this guide and utilizing the available resources, you will be well prepared to take the CKA exam and demonstrate your expertise in Kubernetes.
